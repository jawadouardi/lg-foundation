Our Programs

L&G Foundation operates four core programs that address the most pressing needs of the Knokke-Heist community.

Heritage Property Stewardship

Our Heritage Property Stewardship program is the cornerstone of L&G Foundation's mission. Since our founding in 2008, we have worked tirelessly to identify, document, and protect historically significant properties throughout the Knokke-Heist region. The Belgian coast has experienced rapid development over the past several decades, and without deliberate intervention, many architectural treasures have been lost to neglect, demolition, or insensitive renovation.

Through this program, we conduct comprehensive surveys of heritage properties at risk, working with local architects, historians, and preservation experts to assess the condition and significance of each building. We maintain a detailed database of properties that have been documented, monitored, or restored through our efforts, which now includes over 150 entries spanning residential buildings, public structures, and commercial properties of historical importance.

When we identify a property that requires immediate attention, we coordinate with the building owner, local authorities, and preservation specialists to develop an appropriate intervention strategy. This may involve funding emergency repairs, providing technical guidance for historically accurate restoration, or advocating for legal protection of the property under heritage preservation ordinances.

Over the years, we have successfully contributed to the restoration of 42 heritage properties, ensuring that the architectural character of Knokke-Heist is maintained while allowing these buildings to serve modern purposes. Our approach balances preservation with practicality, recognizing that heritage buildings must remain functional and economically viable to survive in the long term.

Program Highlights

  • 150+ properties documented and monitored
  • 42 properties restored or preserved
  • Annual heritage walk event reaching 200+ participants
  • Partnership with local architectural firms
  • Regular reporting to municipal heritage commission

Community Grants & Philanthropy

The Community Grants & Philanthropy program is how we directly invest in the social fabric of Knokke-Heist and the surrounding Belgian coast region. Since 2013, we have provided financial support to local organizations, community groups, and individuals who share our vision of a stronger, more connected, and more equitable community.

Our grants are awarded through a structured application process that evaluates proposals based on their alignment with our mission, their potential impact, the feasibility of the proposed activities, and the applicant's capacity to deliver results. We prioritize projects that address community needs in the areas of education, social welfare, cultural enrichment, and environmental stewardship.

Over the past decade, we have distributed more than EUR 850,000 in grants to organizations ranging from small grassroots initiatives to established community institutions. Our grant recipients have used these funds to support youth education programs, provide meals and shelter for vulnerable populations, organize cultural festivals and events, and implement environmental conservation projects along the coast.

In addition to direct financial support, we also offer capacity-building assistance to our grant recipients, helping them develop sustainable funding strategies, improve their organizational governance, and measure the impact of their work. This holistic approach ensures that our grants create lasting change rather than providing temporary relief.

We accept grant applications on a rolling basis, with review committees meeting quarterly to evaluate new proposals. All grant decisions are documented and reported in our annual transparency reports, ensuring full accountability for how donor funds are allocated and used.

Program Highlights

  • EUR 850,000+ distributed since 2013
  • 75+ organizations supported
  • Quarterly grant review cycles
  • Capacity-building support for recipients
  • Transparent application and reporting process

Environmental Sustainability

Launched in 2019, our Environmental Sustainability program reflects our deep commitment to protecting the natural environment of the Belgian coast. Knokke-Heist is situated in one of Belgium's most ecologically sensitive regions, where the North Sea coast, the polder landscape, and protected nature reserves create a unique and fragile ecosystem that requires careful stewardship.

Through this program, we fund and support projects that promote sustainable practices, reduce environmental impact, and protect natural habitats along the coast. Our work in this area spans several focus areas, including coastal dune conservation, sustainable energy transitions for community buildings, waste reduction and recycling initiatives, and educational programs on environmental awareness.

We partner with local environmental organizations, municipalities, and schools to implement projects that have both immediate and long-term benefits for the environment. For example, we have funded the restoration of native dune vegetation along vulnerable stretches of coastline, supported the installation of solar panels on community buildings, and organized beach cleanup events that engage hundreds of volunteers each year.

Our environmental programs are guided by the principle that heritage preservation and environmental sustainability are deeply interconnected. The cultural landscape of Knokke-Heist, including its historic buildings, traditional land use patterns, and natural features, represents a holistic heritage that must be preserved in its entirety. By protecting the environment, we also protect the context in which our cultural heritage exists and thrives.

Program Highlights

  • Dune restoration projects along 3km of coastline
  • Solar installations on 8 community buildings
  • Annual beach cleanup with 200+ volunteers
  • Environmental education in 5 local schools
  • Partnership with Flemish Nature Agency

Cultural Education & Awareness

Our Cultural Education & Awareness program, established in 2022, is the newest addition to L&G Foundation's portfolio of initiatives. This program was created in response to growing demand from the community for accessible, engaging educational opportunities that celebrate the rich cultural history of the Knokke-Heist region and inspire a deeper appreciation for heritage and the arts.

Through this program, we organize a diverse range of educational activities, including heritage walks through historic neighborhoods, lectures by local historians and architects, workshops on traditional crafts and building techniques, and cultural festivals that bring the community together to celebrate our shared identity. Each year, we aim to reach at least 300 participants through our educational programming.

We place particular emphasis on youth engagement, developing age-appropriate educational materials and activities that introduce children and teenagers to the history and heritage of their community. Our school partnership program provides teachers with resources and support to integrate heritage education into their curricula, and we regularly host school groups for guided heritage walks and interactive workshops.

We also work to raise awareness of heritage preservation issues among the general public, using our cultural events and educational programs as platforms to advocate for thoughtful development practices and respect for the architectural and natural heritage of our region. By fostering a culture of appreciation and awareness, we aim to build a community that values and protects its heritage for generations to come.

All of our educational programs are offered free of charge or at minimal cost to participants, ensuring that financial barriers do not prevent anyone from engaging with their cultural heritage. We fund these activities through a combination of grants, donations, and support from our community partners.

Program Highlights

  • 300+ participants in annual educational programs
  • Partnerships with 5 local schools
  • Monthly heritage walks (March–November)
  • Annual Cultural Heritage Festival
  • Free or low-cost participation for all
